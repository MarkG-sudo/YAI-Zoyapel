Extracting Bromelain from Pineapple Waste

Turning waste into value for pharmaceutical and cosmetic industries

About the Product

Bromelain is an enzyme extracted from pineapple stems, used to reduce pain and swelling. Every year, thousands of tons of pineapple stems go to waste after fruit processing. Yet, hidden within these discarded stems lies a powerful enzyme—bromelain—renowned for its anti-inflammatory, pain-relieving, and digestive benefits.

Our innovation extracts and purifies bromelain from pineapple waste, transforming an overlooked by-product into a high-value ingredient for the pharmaceutical, nutraceutical, and cosmetics industries.

About the Innovator

Douglas Akolgo, a 35-year-old entrepreneur from Agona East in Ghana’s Central Region, is transforming pineapple waste into a valuable health solution.

Growing up in a farming community, he witnessed firsthand how much of the pineapple harvest was discarded after processing. Determined to create something meaningful from this waste, he developed an innovative method to extract and purify bromelain from pineapple stems.
